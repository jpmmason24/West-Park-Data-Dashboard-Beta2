West Park High School
CA Dashboard Analysis · 2022-23 to 2024-25

Three years of California School Dashboard data. Real momentum, sharper gaps.

West Park High has lifted overall academic placement on the California School Dashboard from a mixed 2023 picture to a Green-and-Blue 2024-25 — yet beneath the headline gains, the school's Hispanic/Latino, English Learner, and Students with Disabilities subgroups have moved in the opposite direction. This dashboard distills the trend, the disparities, and the specific work for the year ahead.

2025–2026 Focus areas

Seven priorities, ordered by urgency

Each card pairs the dashboard evidence with concrete actions — drawing on CDE placements, CAASPP/ELPAC pass rates, and SARC-reported subgroup performance. Now that we have richer subgroup data, the priorities below are updated and re-ranked.

01
Critical · only Red on the board

English Learner suspension rate

Evidence: 2024-25 placement is Red — Very High status (10.1%+) with Increased change. SARC confirms an actual rate of 10.81% versus 3.32% schoolwide. The trajectory: Orange (2022-23, Decl.) → Green (2023-24, Decl.) → Red (2024-25, Increased). Population context: ~77 ELs total, so the numerator is small — roughly 8 students suspended at least once. A focused case-management approach can move this indicator.

  • Individual case review for every EL suspended in 2024-25 — language proficiency level, ELPAC band, course schedule, infraction category. Disaggregate by LTEL vs. newcomer.
  • EL-specific tier-2 supports in MTSS — restorative conferencing with bilingual mediation, EL family engagement before suspension is used, designated EL counselor caseload.
  • Implicit bias / cultural responsiveness PD for staff with high referral volume of EL students.
  • Monthly suspension monitoring reported to admin team and DELAC; target return to Yellow status (≤6%) by mid-year.
02
Critical · 82% of ELs not yet proficient

English Learner Progress & ELPAC reclassification

Evidence: ELPI placement is Orange — Medium status (45–54.9% making progress) with Declined change. ELPAC 2024-25 distribution: 35.4% Level 1 (Beginning to Develop), 17.2% L2, 29.3% L3, only 18.2% Level 4 (the reclassification standard). CAASPP pass rates for ELs are the lowest on the dashboard: 9.7% ELA, 9.7% Math, 2.0% Science. Combined with the suspension Red, this is a system-level signal that EL programming needs investment.

  • ELD program audit — designated and integrated ELD scheduling, alignment to ELD standards, instructional minutes, and walkthrough fidelity.
  • Long-Term EL (LTEL) intervention plan — specific course placement and reclassification monitoring; a third of ELs at Level 1 suggests a stuck pipeline.
  • ELPAC subdomain analysis — identify which of Reading / Writing / Listening / Speaking is the weakest and target accordingly. Move L3 students toward L4 reclassification eligibility.
  • Hire/reassign an EL TOSA or coordinator if not already in place to own the indicator.
03
Critical · upgraded from High

Students with Disabilities — academic preparation across all three subjects

Evidence: SwD reached reportable size in 2024-25 and immediately landed Orange on graduation rate (Low status, Declined). The SARC pass rates published this year change the picture entirely: 14.3% ELA, 9.5% Math, 6.1% Science Met or Exceeded — gaps of 56, 36, and 47 percentage points below All Students. Graduation 77.8%; suspension 8.39% (2.5× school average). On CCI, 63.9% are Not Prepared. This is now the largest single-subgroup academic gap on the dashboard. Population: ~127 students (6.1% of enrollment).

  • Co-teaching model audit for general-ed access in core ELA, Math, and Science courses — the simultaneous gap across all three subjects suggests a structural rather than course-specific cause.
  • Graduation cohort tracking for current SwD seniors and juniors — credit checks, A–G progress, IEP transition goals.
  • CTE pathway access for SwD — currently 44.4% of Prepared SwD use this pathway; expand to more students.
  • Transition Classwork & Work-Based Learning measure shows 0% — for IEP students earning a Special Ed Certificate, this is an underused CCI pathway worth considering.
04
High · academic gap across three subjects

Hispanic/Latino ELA, Math & Science performance

Evidence: Hispanic/Latino students post the school's largest CCI gain (50% → 66.7%, +16.7pp) but their CAASPP performance shows a different story: 64.6% ELA, 33.9% Math, 45.0% Science Met or Exceeded — gaps of 6, 12, and 8 percentage points below All Students. ELA placement volatility tells the same story (Yellow Medium / Decl. Sig. in 2024-25 after a Blue High peak in 2023-24). Math regressed Green to Orange. Population: 15.9% of enrollment (~330 students).

  • Item-level CAASPP diagnostic review across all three subjects — which standards drove the score swings?
  • 11th-grade SBAC prep cycle — targeted small-group intervention in the 6 weeks before testing window.
  • Bridge the gap between course-grade success (which fuels CCI) and assessment performance — examine grade-to-test alignment.
  • Hispanic/Latino family engagement series on academic standards expectations, especially in Math and Science.
05
High · largest equity group · trend reversal

Socioeconomically Disadvantaged · College/Career trajectory

Evidence: SED is the school's largest equity group at 30.1% of enrollment (~626 students). Strong academic-placement gains (ELA Orange→Green w/ Inc. Sig.; Math Orange→Green w/ Inc. Sig.) — but on CCI they have moved 54.4% → 51.2% → 48.9% Prepared, holding Orange (Medium × Declined) for two cycles. CAASPP gaps remain: 58.9% ELA, 41.8% Math, 38.85% Science Met or Exceeded. Graduation is solid at 94.9% (Very High band).

  • Counselor-led A–G monitoring for SED juniors — the SARC reports 88.5% of SED meet UC/CSU; the gap is the ~30% who fall to Not Prepared on CCI.
  • Free dual-enrollment push — College Credit Course is only 3.9% of all-student preparedness; SED students benefit most from cost-free college credit.
  • AP exam fee waivers communicated to all SED families; 49.4% AP completion is solid but exam-take rates among SED should be tracked.
  • Senior-year case management for SED students at risk of falling from Approaching to Not Prepared.
06
High · small-N subgroups easy to overlook

Smaller subgroups — Black/AA, Two or More Races, Homeless

Evidence: Three populations the new SARC data brings into focus, all with concerning patterns hidden by their small enrollment shares:
· Black/AA (4.2%, ~87 students): 23.1% Math, 32.0% Science Met or Exceeded — both well below All Students. Suspension 8.33%. Graduation 100% (every cohort grad).
· Two or More Races (6.6%, ~137 students): 11.76% Math (lowest non-EL), 78.6% graduation (third-lowest), suspension Increased Significantly two years running.
· Homeless (0.8%, ~17 students): 15.79% suspension, 44.4% chronic absenteeism per SARC.

  • Quarterly small-subgroup review — these populations are too small to anchor an indicator color but easily moved by individual case management.
  • Black Student Union / Affinity supports — academic mentoring focused on Math and Science, plus the discipline trend.
  • Two or More Races trajectory check — the suspension Inc. Sig. for two years is the strongest single-direction trend among small subgroups; root-cause it.
  • Homeless liaison + McKinney-Vento — confirm transportation, meals, and case management; the absenteeism rate suggests barriers are not all academic.
07
Medium · ceiling slipping

Asian student CCI ceiling

Evidence: Asian students lead the school in academic placement (Blue ELA, Blue Math, both Increased Sig.) and post strong CAASPP rates (72.4% ELA, 55.2% Math). But CCI Prepared has eroded 78.4% → 70.5% → 63.2% — a 15.2-point drop in two years that moved placement from Green Very High to Yellow High. Population: 16.2% of enrollment (~337 students).

  • Course-taking pattern analysis — are Asian students choosing fewer AP/CTE sequences, or completing UC/CSU but missing the secondary criteria?
  • Senior-exit survey on post-secondary intent vs. CCI capture; some declines reflect students choosing pathways the indicator doesn't measure cleanly.
  • SBAC fidelity — Asian SBAC met rate is solid but check year-over-year for engagement signals.
★
Wins to protect

What's working — keep doing this

Evidence: Four patterns to preserve in the year ahead because losing them would unwind the school's overall trajectory.

  • Math schoolwide trajectory — 38% → 46% Met or Exceeded year-over-year is the dashboard's strongest single move, paired with placement Inc. Sig. twice in a row.
  • SED academic intervention — whatever the school did between 2022-23 and 2023-24 to flip ELA and Math from Orange to Green w/ Increased Significantly is the equity story of the dashboard. Document and protect it.
  • Hispanic/Latino college pathway — +16.7pp on CCI in two years. Keep the counselors, the A–G push, and the family communication that produced it.
  • UC/CSU completion at 87.9% — Level 4 (highest) for every reportable subgroup including EL (85.7%), SED (88.5%), and SwD (77.8%). And 98.5% of students are enrolled in A–G courses (per SARC). This is the school's quiet superpower.
2025–2026 implementation roadmap

What gets done, when, who watches it

Three windows — Fall, Winter, Spring — each with the priority work, the indicator it serves, and the cadence for monitoring. Updated to reflect the seven-priority structure including SwD academics (upgraded to critical), small-subgroup sweep, and ELPAC reclassification pipeline.

2025–2026 Plan

From data to delivery

A school year sequenced around the dashboard. Every action ties back to a measurable indicator.

Fall · Aug–Oct

Diagnose & deploy

  • EL suspension case-by-case review · individual case management for the ~8 ELs suspended in 2024-25
  • ELD program audit · designated/integrated minutes, ELPAC subdomain analysis, L3 → L4 reclassification pipeline
  • SwD co-teaching audit · ELA/Math/Science access verification, given the 56/36/47 pp pass-rate gaps
  • Hispanic/Latino three-subject item analysis · 2024-25 CAASPP standards-level review across ELA, Math, and Science
  • Small-subgroup baseline review · Black/AA, Two or More Races, Homeless — establish individual-student case lists
  • SED senior cohort A–G snapshot · who is on track, who is at risk of Not Prepared
Winter · Nov–Feb

Intervene & adjust

  • Tier-2 EL behavior supports live · monthly DELAC report-out
  • 11th-grade SBAC prep cycle · Hispanic/Latino + SwD + SED target groups
  • SwD CTE pathway expansion · enroll more SwD in CTE sequences before spring registration
  • Black/AA Math & Science academic mentoring · paired with departmental math support
  • AP/IB exam registration push · fee waivers communicated to all SED & underrepresented families
  • SED dual-enrollment registration window · expand on the 3.9% baseline of College Credit Course completion
  • Mid-year color projection · run all seven indicators against 2026 placement
Spring · Mar–Jun

Test, finish, capture

  • CAASPP / ELPAC testing window · proctored conditions, accommodations validated, ELPAC reclassification candidates identified
  • Senior CCI capture · UC/CSU verifications, Seal of Biliteracy testing, CTE capstone grades, Special Ed Certificate transition pathway
  • Discipline data freeze · final EL suspension rate vs. baseline; small-subgroup discipline check
  • Reclassification ceremony · ELPAC L4 students celebrated; reclassification rate published
  • End-of-year indicator forecast · what placement does the school expect in November 2026?
  • SPSA refresh · 2026–27 priorities staged before fall
